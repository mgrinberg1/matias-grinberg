I was born in Chicago but I've lived most of my life in Buenos Aires. When I started building Queiros, a lot of people assumed I'd eventually move the company to the US. I haven't, and I don't plan to.

Here's why Argentina is actually a great place to build right now — and where the real challenges are.

## The talent is real

Buenos Aires has deep engineering talent. The universities produce serious people, and years of economic instability have created a generation of builders who are resourceful by necessity. You learn to do more with less. That's a good trait in a startup.

## The market is underserved

Most B2B software is built for companies in the US or Western Europe. The workflows, the assumptions, the pricing — all of it assumes a certain kind of customer. Operations teams across Latin America are running complex, real-world logistics with tools that weren't built for them. That's a gap worth closing.

## What's actually hard

The honest answer: currency complexity, banking friction, and the mental load of operating across time zones and regulatory environments. None of it is insurmountable. But it requires more overhead than building in a single-market context.

## Nuñez as a base

I live in Nuñez, in the north of Buenos Aires. It's calm, close to the river, and has good coffee. A lot of good thinking happens on walks here.

If you're ever passing through Buenos Aires and want to talk startups, operations, or River Plate — find me.
