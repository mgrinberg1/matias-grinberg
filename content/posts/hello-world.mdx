I've spent a lot of time around operations teams — logistics, construction, agriculture — and one thing is always the same: the people doing the actual work in the field are forced to adapt to tools built for people sitting at a desk.

Rigid forms. Manual spreadsheets. Data that lives in someone's WhatsApp and never makes it into a system.

The irony is that everyone already has the perfect tool in their pocket: their phone. And in Latin America, that phone almost certainly has WhatsApp open all day.

## The insight

What if capturing structured data was as easy as sending a voice note?

That's the core idea behind Queiros. You define what information you need — think of it like setting up a table with columns — and your field team just sends messages the way they already do. Audio, photos, documents. The AI handles turning that into clean, structured records.

No training required. No new app to install. No one staring at a form wondering what to fill in.

## What we learned early

The first thing we learned is that the format of the data matters less than the habit. If people are already sending voice notes, let them send voice notes. Don't ask them to change behavior — meet them where they are.

The second thing: reports shouldn't require a data analyst. The whole point of collecting field data is to make decisions. Queiros generates those insights automatically.
